CONSUL SUTD is a participation tool adopted to use in the SUTD Campus Transformation project. It enables a web-based platform of communication, which allows students and campus users to make proposals for action to be supported by other users, express their decision on particularly significant issues, participate in the process of developing ideas or decide on the destination of the existing processes.

 

1        PARTICIPATION PROCESS

The participation process aims to collect the relevant information from the users of a specific area before making changes and modifications. In this case, the Transform the Campus project focuses on the Singapore East Coast Development, which is an urban transformation project including the SUTD campus, Changi Business Park, and Singapore Expo area. We would like to understand the potentials of each area and their relationships to create a holistic approach towards the East Coast area. While doing that, we put SUTD Campus to the center to seek ways to increase the vibrancy of the campus and understand the campus users opinions of the current situation and possible scenarios.

Below, you will find the instructions about the tool, Consul SUTD, to help you to understand how you can participate in the processes. There is no limit to commenting, proposing, or voting! So please use the tool as effectively as possible to contribute to the SUTD Campus Transformation Project!

3        PARTICIPATION MODULES

 

When you sign in, you will see three main sections on the top of your screen (Figure 2). A map-based tool is also embedded into the homepage, which you can reach by clicking the Map your Ideas button (Figure 8).

 

Figure 2. The participation modules within Consul SUTD.

 

3.1       Debates

 

Users can open threads of discussion on any topic and create independent spaces where they can debate and give their opinion on a specific topic. The debates module makes it easier for users to make visible the issues that seem important to them, and for them to meet each other to debate or collaborate on these issues. It is a space for listening, but also for gathering.

In order to participate in an existing debate, write your comment and publish it (Figure 3). You can answer the comments of your colleagues as well. Moreover, users can vote for or against the debates, so that the most highly valued debates are those that are regularly displayed on the main debates page as seen in the figure below. All discussions have a comments section, where users can debate them with each other. Comments are also voted for or against.

 

Figure 3. Publish comment and support sections within debate module.

If you would like to start a debate, you can easily click on the start a debate button on the top right of your screen (Figure 4.). After that, you will need to follow the instructions and post your debate topic to discuss with your colleagues.

 

Figure 4. Start a debate button in the debate module.

3.2       Proposals

 

The proposals section allows anyone to submit proposals. These proposals gather support up to a decided threshold to select the most relevant ones. When they reach this threshold, the proposals can be moved to the voting section, where a vote can be taken for or against the proposal, or directly considered to be carried out.

Proposals can include tags, images, documentation, videos, location on a map, or other relevant information. It also has a comment space where anyone can discuss the proposal, as well as vote on other comments to select the most relevant ones.

You can support the existing proposals by using the support button. (More support will make your proposal more visible!) If you like to create your own proposal, you can use the Create a proposal button on the top right of your screen as in Figure 5.

 

Figure 5. Support a proposal or create a new proposal in proposal module.

In this module, you can locate your proposal on a map, or use the additional map-tools provided in some of the proposal descriptions as shown in Figure 6.

 

Figure 6. Embedded maps in Proposal Module.

3.3       Voting

The voting module allows voting on users proposals or specific questions that are raised by the authorities. As stated above, the supported proposals are voted in this module. Therefore, if you would like to create a new poll, you should create a new proposal and get enough support to create a voting poll for it. Alternatively, if you think that you have an interesting topic to create a poll in this module, you can contact the Principal Investigator through email to start a new one.

There are some existing polls that you can directly participate in. In these cases, you can directly use the button next to it and answer the questions (Figure 7). You are highly encouraged to participate in the existing polls as well as to create your own proposals to get enough support for this module.

Figure 7. Participate in this poll button in the voting module.

3.4       Map

The last section of this participation tool is a map-based interface to ask your opinions and suggestion on a map. The access is through the link in the featured section on the homepage (Figure 8). There are a number of embedded maps in the proposal and debates modules as well as explained above (Figure 6).

Figure 8. Access to the map module in Consul SUTD.

A legend is already provided for each map, so please follow it and add as much explanation as possible for designers to understand your suggestions/comments (Figure 9). As the map is a shared interface, please do not delete your colleagues' data, and please do not forget to save the map after you finish editing (Figure 10).
